CONCEPT NOTE

This conference aims to chart the path towards a developed India by 2047, focusing on key themes such as Economic Growth, Social Inclusion, Environmental Sustainability, Technological Advancement, Governance Reform, Cultural Preservation, and Global Leadership. Through interactive discussions and expert insights, we seek to identify actionable strategies and policies that foster inclusive growth, preserve our heritage, and position India as a global leader in the 21st century.

THIS CONFERENCE AIMS TO–

  • ➤  Explore strategies to boost job creation and entrepreneurship.
  • ➤  Discuss policies for inclusive economic growth, ensuring benefits reach all segments of society.
  • ➤  Identify barriers to equal opportunities and propose solutions for marginalized communities.
  • ➤  Share best practices for promoting gender equality and social justice.
  • ➤  Address the challenges of climate change and pollution through sustainable practices.
  • ➤  Advocate for conservation of natural resources and biodiversity.
  • ➤  Showcase innovations that improve lives and drive economic progress.
  • ➤  Discuss policies to bridge the digital divide and promote technology for social good.
  • ➤  Explore ways to enhance transparency, accountability, and efficiency in governance.
  • ➤  Propose reforms to strengthen institutions and uphold the rule of law.
  • ➤  Highlight the importance of preserving diverse cultural heritage for future generations.
  • ➤  Discuss strategies for promoting cultural exchange and intercultural understanding.
  • ➤  Evaluate India's role in global affairs and its potential for leadership.
  • ➤  Discuss diplomatic initiatives to promote peace, cooperation, and mutual understanding among nations.

THEMES AND SUB-THEMES

  • A. Economic Growth and Prosperity:
    Industry diversification, infrastructure development, job creation.

  • B. Social Inclusion and Equity:
    Education accessibility, healthcare affordability, gender equality.

  • C. Environmental Sustainability:
    Renewable energy adoption, biodiversity conservation, waste management.

  • D. Technological Advancement and Innovation:
    Digitalization, research and development, entrepreneurship.

  • E. Governance and Institutional Reform:
    Transparency, accountability, judicial reform.

  • F. Cultural Heritage and Preservation:
    Heritage sites preservation, cultural exchange programs, traditional arts promotion.

  • G. Global Leadership and Diplomacy:
    International cooperation, peacekeeping efforts, soft power diplomacy.

  • H. Indian Knowledge System:
    Preserve and Promote Traditional Knowledge, Tribal Practices, Artistic Expressions.

  • I. Internal Security:
    Naxalism, Cyber Security, Data Protection, Media and Propaganda Warfare.

Author Guidelines for Abstract and Full Paper Submission

The abstract should not exceed 250-300 words. It should include 4-6 alphabetically arranged keywords. The full length paper should be of 4000-6000 words that should include Abstract, keywords, Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology, Findings and Discussion, Conclusion and References. All the papers should be in Times New Roman, font size 12 with 1.5 line spacing. All the papers should follow APA-7/MLA-9 for Referencing.

Publication

The authors are requested to submit their full paper only as word document through e-mail to: vvbnc2024@gmail.com before the last date. All the papers will be included in the conference proceedings with ISBN as E-Book. Selected papers will be published in the UGC / ABDC / Scopus indexed journals with applicable charges determined by the respective journals.
